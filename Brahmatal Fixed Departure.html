Overview

Brahmatal Fixed Departure Group Tour ex-Dehradun/Rishikesh

Brahmatal trek offers to change vistas, from deep and shadowy forests of oaks and rhododendrons to snow-covered meadows, lakeside camping at Bekaltal, the alpine Brahmatal lake vicinity, and a climactic vision of fabulous high peaks glittering across a brilliant sky. The Brahmatal Trek route is fit for beginners and less experienced trekkers. A major part of the trek continues under forest cover, diminishing the chances of altitude sickness.

  • 06 Days 05 Nights
  • Age 12+
  • Uttrakhand

Short Itinerary:

  • Day 1 :Dehradun to Lohajung
  • Day 2 :Lohajung to Bekaltal
  • Day 3 :Bekaltal to Brahmatal
  • Day 4 :Brahmatal to Tilbudi via Brahmatal Pass
  • Day 5 :Tilbudi to Lohajung via Wan
  • Day 6 :Lohajung to Dehradun

    Trek Itinerary

    01

    Day 1: Dehradun to Lohajung


    Drive 296km Stay Lodge

    The pickup place is Dehradun railway station early in the morning. The journey uphill from Dehradun to Lohajung will take you cruising through mountain alleyways connecting the lower Himalayan belt of Garhwal hills to that of Garhwal. This is going to be a 10 hour drive, despite the marvelous changing vistas of the mountains. For the first few hours, the course of our journey will be running parallel to river Ganga and Alaknanda, flowing softly. Soon, a blustery Pindar river will meet our path.

    Pick up time 06:00 am at the Dehradun railway station. Exact time you will be updated by our Trek Leader.

    Route: Dehradun – Rishikesh – Devprayag – Srinagar – Rudraprayag – Karnaprayag – Lohajung.

    02

    Day 2: Lohajung to Bekaltal


    Trek 6km Stay Camps

    We will start the trek after breakfast. The first day’s trek spanning 5 hours will kick off from Lohajung up a snaky trail going into the forest. After a short while, the forest will give way to a small village named Mandoli to your left. Take approximately an hour’s hike after crossing the last stream to reach the beautiful Bekaltal gleaming peacefully in the summer while partially frozen over in the winters.

    03

    Day 3: Bekaltal to Brahmatal


    Trek 7km Stay Camps

    Today’s trek will take you through thickets of oaks and rhododendrons over steep slopes for quite some time before finally coming out to a meadow. This is the meadow of Telandi. In the winter, you will find the green expanse of Telandi transform into a vast snowfield and crossing the area, a gala of thrill.

    04

    Day 4: Brahmatal – Brahmatal Pass – Tilbudi


    Trek 7km Stay Camps

    Start your day early in the morning as you will be heading towards the Brahmatal lake which is an uphill climb. You will be greeted with Oak and Rhododendron trees on the way which have a charm of their own. After a while, the zigzag ascent trail will take you Brahmatal Lake. After walking for a while and crossing the meadow, the final climb towards the pass begins.

    It will be a tiring walk towards but as soon as you reach the top you will be greeted with a magnificent view of Mt. Trishul and Mt. Nanda Ghunti. Take your time and click some jaw-dropping pictures that will last a lifetime. As soon as you start descending, you discover that the trail is different from that which got you up. As you continue to descend, after an hour you will reach a clearing surrounded by trees on all the sides which is the Tilbudi campsite.

    05

    Day 5: Tilbudi – Wan – Lohajung


    Trek 4km Drive 25km

    After having your breakfast you will start your descent to Wan which is a trail filled with Oak and Rhododendron trees. This is a very beautiful trail which allows you to click the end number of scenic pictures. After walking some distance you will come across a water source at Chota Tilbundi. Half an hour later you will be able to spot Wan village which is visible below. From Wan we will take transport to reach Lohajung. Night stay in Lohajung.

    06

    Day 6: Lohajung to Dehradun


    Drive 296km

    Departure from Lohajung after breakfast. We will reach Dehradun in the evening. Trekking services will end here.
